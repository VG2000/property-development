Conwy County – Investor Brief

Connectivity, demand drivers, and priorities shaping the visitor economy.

Why Conwy?

Conwy sits at the heart of North Wales with sea–mountain geography, quick access to the A55/A470, and rail via Llandudno Junction to London in ~3 hours. Within a 3-hour drive it captures most major North-West England conurbations—fuel for strong day and short-break markets.

Tourism is a cornerstone sector: ~7.8m visitors generated an estimated £729m in 2021 (STEAM), rebounding from pandemic effects (2019: ~£996m). Staying visitors (~1.61m) punch above their weight—nearly double the economic impact of day trippers.

Demand & Accommodation

Llandudno is the serviced-accommodation hub of North Wales with ~15,000 beds per night; the East coast holds significant ~50,000 non-serviced (static) beds. The county offers a broad mix—hotels, self-catering, holiday parks, hostels and camping—supporting diverse price points and stay patterns.

Core drivers: coastline (45 miles; 17.5% Heritage Coast), Eryri landscapes (38% of county in the park), World Heritage (Conwy Castle & Town Walls), adventure attractions (Surf Snowdonia, Zip World), and marquee venues (Venue Cymru) and gardens (Bodnant).

Connectivity & Catchment

The A55 links directly to the M56 (Manchester/Liverpool airports). Rail connectivity and proximity to Holyhead strengthen Ireland–UK flows. The 3-hour road catchment underpins resilient domestic demand for day trips and short stays year-round.

What the County is Targeting (2023–2029)

  • Grow year-round value (events in shoulder months, stronger evening economy, food offer).
  • Quality & sustainability: cleaner, better facilities; greener transport; data-led decisions.
  • Manage peak pressure & spread visitors to lesser-known areas; promote active travel.
  • Skills & workforce: link business with training; utilise the Conwy Ambassador scheme.
  • Plan-led enablement: align with the Local Development Plan to support tourism investment.

Source: Destination Conwy Management Plan 2023–2029.

Investor Angles

  • Serviced & Aparthotel near Llandudno/Conwy: taps robust coastal demand and events.
  • Holiday parks & high-quality self-catering (upgrade/amenities) on the East coast belt.
  • Town-centre mixed-use & F&B to strengthen evening economy and shoulder season trade.
  • Experience-led attractions aligned to adventure/coastal and cultural heritage themes.
  • Green mobility & infrastructure that supports the DMP’s sustainability goals.

Inferences based on DMP objectives and product mix outlined in the plan.
